In 2002 I was shown a vision of a Bible. A vision of a completely interactive, immersive, cross-platform (from virtual reality to mobile gaming), online, "multiplayer" Bible.  I was then told to wait on the Lord’s timing, and to pursue a career that would prepare me for this. A few months ago the Lord started prompting me to step out and start this project.  I've since tendered my resignation as a software developer at Amazon Web Services to give this my “full-time” attention.

The basic premise is this:  The Bible is the inerrant, authoritative, word of God, and it is almost exclusively a book.  But fewer and fewer people are reading.  While I believe this is a tragedy, it is the fact.  As of October 2017, the full Bible has been translated into 670 languages, however if we and our children are not consuming the medium, it doesn’t help whatever language you speak.

To that end, the Lord showed me a vision of a Bible which will appeal to an age that consumes digital content.  Studies are showing that esports are more popular amongst millennials than regular sports.  The computer gaming industry is huge, and the internet is at 81% of the developed world’s fingertips.  The message of the Bible must not change, but it’s medium needs to be able to reach the people.  This is a Bible translation like no other.  It’s a translation into the language of the digital consumer.

I was given this vision in 2002 by the Lord.  At that time I was in the 10th grade.  The Lord then very clearly told me to wait on His timing, and to pursue a career that would prepare me for this vision.

He directed me into Computer Science, and then more specifically into Computer Game and Simulation Design. Once I’d finished my studies I went into the software development industry, working for three years at a software development house.  During this time I got married to the love of my life.

We then spent six months in Israel, during which time this vision grew.  However the Lord continued to say not yet quite loudly.

When we moved back to Cape Town, I took up a post at Amazon Web Services.  While I was very grateful to the Lord for the job, I was disappointed that it still wasn’t time to work on this project.  However I had peace that I was where the Lord wanted me.

That was almost four years ago, and what I’ve learned in that time, and how I’ve grown as a developer at Amazon, where we deal daily with how to develop software so that it can reach everyone and grow to handle the load, has prepared me in ways I wasn’t even aware of before then.  I praise the Lord for my time at Amazon, because it has made this project possible and has taught me much that would have caused us issues down the line.

During those four years, my wife and I bought our first house, and became a mommy and daddy.  My family is such a joy and blessing.

I was beginning to feel that the project was never to be, having to support a family and pay a bond doesn’t leave the option of just up and quitting.

And then the Lord changed everything.  A few months ago I felt the Lord’s constant sense of “this is not the time” disappear.  It was this sudden release of pressure which I hadn’t even felt building until it suddenly wasn’t there any more.  I was no longer waiting on the Lord, He was waiting on me.  A short while later I lost the peace that I had about being at Amazon.

I began praying about whether or not to leave Amazon, and that the Lord would make it abundantly clear to me, as I didn’t want to leave without being able to support my family.  Well the Lord answered that prayer.  The Lord provided me with a couple of months worth of finances out of the blue a few days later.  I’ve never had the money needed to take a one or two months off to pursue this and attempt to raise supportive funding needed to do this full time.

I have now tendered my resignation from Amazon.  My final day there is the 16th of May.

My prayer is that if it is not Lord’s will that this project is to happen now, that He enables me to see it as soon as possible.  But that if it is His will that it starts now, that He will lay it on the correct peoples’ hearts to commit to supporting this project.
