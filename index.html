I was given this vision in 2002 by the Lord.  At that time I was in the 10th grade.  The Lord then very clearly told me to wait on His timing, and to pursue a career that would prepare me for this vision.

 

I presented this vision to my dad (a long time Bible teacher), who immediately recognised it as an extension of a vision given him years before.  His vision was to create a timeline of Scripture, and have models and explanations all set up in a way that could be packed up and taken from location to location, to show that Scripture is so much more than just dry words on a page.

 

The Lord directed me into Computer Science, and then more specifically into Computer Game and Simulation Design. Once I’d finished my studies I went into the software development industry, working for three years at a software development house.  During this time I got married to the love of my life.

 

We then spent six months in Israel, during which time this vision grew.  However the Lord continued to say not yet quite loudly.

 

When we moved back to Cape Town, I took up a post at Amazon Web Services.  While I was very grateful to the Lord for the job, I was disappointed that it still wasn’t time to work on this project.  However I had peace that I was where the Lord wanted me.

 

That was almost four years ago, and what I’ve learned in that time, and how I’ve grown as a developer at Amazon, where we deal daily with how to develop software so that it can reach everyone and grow to handle the load, has prepared me in ways I wasn’t even aware of before then.  I praise the Lord for my time at Amazon, because it has made this project possible and has taught me much that would have caused us issues down the line.

 

During those four years, my wife and I bought our first house, and became a mommy and daddy.  My family is such a joy and blessing.

 

I was beginning to feel that the project was never to be, having to support a family and pay a bond doesn’t leave the option of just up and quitting.

 

And then the Lord changed everything.  A few months ago I felt the Lord’s constant sense of “this is not the time” disappear.  It was this sudden release of pressure which I hadn’t even felt building until it suddenly wasn’t there any more.  I was no longer waiting on the Lord, He was waiting on me.  A short while later I lost the peace that I had about being at Amazon.

 

I began praying about whether or not to leave Amazon, and that the Lord would make it abundantly clear to me, as I didn’t want to leave without being able to support my family.  Well the Lord answered that prayer.  The Lord provided me with a couple of months worth of finances out of the blue a few days later.  I’ve never had the money needed to take a one or two months off to pursue this and attempt to raise supportive funding needed to do this full time.

 

I have now tendered my resignation from Amazon.  My final day there is the 18th of May.

 

My dad and I are going into this together, under the leadership of the Lord, and the authority of His Word, to create this Bible.

 

Our prayer is that if it is not Lord’s will that this project is to happen now, that He enables us to see it as soon as possible.  But that if it is His will that it starts now, that He will lay it on the correct peoples’ hearts to commit to supporting this project.

The Bible is the inerrant, authoritative, Word of God. It is the world's all time best seller, and also a candidate for the least read book. Fewer and fewer people are reading. While we believe that this is a tragedy, it is the fact. As of October 2017, the full Bible has been translated into 670 languages, however if we and our children are not consuming the medium, it doesn’t help whatever language you speak.

 

What is being consumed is digital media.  Studies are showing that esports are more popular amongst millennials than regular sports.  The computer gaming industry is huge, and the internet is at 81% of the developed world’s fingertips.  The message of the Bible must not change, but it’s medium needs to be able to reach the people.

 

The Way Walkers' Ministries is here to address that problem. Come and join us as we set out on our journey to create this free Bible.   This is a Bible translation like no other.

It’s a translation into the language of the digital consumer.

 

In The Way Walkers, readers (“players” are referred to as readers, as they are consuming Scripture, not playing a game) will be able to walk through a digital representation of the places and times of the Bible.  Great care and much research will go into ensuring that everything is as close to authentic as research will allow.  And as new research uncovers new cultural knowledge, this will be incorporated into the program.

 

Full immersion, with as many senses as possible engaged is the most profound method of ensuring that content remains sealed in someone’s memory.  As such The Way Walkers will reach its readers on every level available.  Be it the screen and tinny speakers of a mobile phone, or a rich immersive full room virtual reality environment.

 

Readers will be able to select a location, time period, and/or passage of scripture, to be transported to that place and time.  They will be able to witness the progression of Mount Zion, being transformed from a small town, witnessing an elderly man almost sacrifice his son.  King David conquering the city.  Solomon building the temple.  The rise and fall of kings, some good, many bad.  The destruction of the Temple by the Babylonians.  The rebuilding of the Temple, and a few prophets, including Ezra and Nehemiah spurring that effort along.  They will see a baby first born boy being redeemed at the Temple, and a righteous man named Simeon praising the Lord.  They will see a twelve year old boy amazing the teachers with his questions.  They will see a man with twelve disciples enter the city on a donkey, and a few days later be crucified, only to rise again, and soon after be taken into heaven…  And that’s just one city.

 

Readers will be able to select specific roles to play (with obvious care taken to ensure that nothing inappropriate can be done by the player given each role).  Alternately they will be able to view as a spectator, or see a birds eye view.

 

Scripture, cross references, explanations on how what they are seeing and interacting with fulfills prophecy laid down centuries beforehand, will all be poured into them as the readers interact with the Sacred Text in this new format.

 

A few years ago I spent six months in Israel.  One of the most remarkable things about being in Israel with other believers is that you are constantly talking about Scripture and praying together.  The reason this happens is because you are literally there.  You can’t help but talk about Scripture when you wake up to a view of the Galilee every morning, Capernaum, the Mount of Beatitudes, the lake itself, it’s all right in front of you.  And that is what we want to foster in The Way Walkers.  Readers will be able to interact with each other, discuss new findings, go exploring together, share knowledge and resources.  Naturally the interactive side will be parent lockable.

 

We have so many more ideas on what we want for this project.  But the above is more than enough to start with.
